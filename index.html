01 / Bio

Student lens, professional rigor.

I'm a final-year Biomedical Engineering student at Selvam College of Technology, working at the intersection of medical hardware and intelligent systems. My focus is simple: equipment that's reliable when it matters most, and patients who are monitored with insight, not just instruments.

What started as classroom theory turned into hands-on hospital floors — observing critical care units, servicing diagnostic devices, and learning that great biomedical engineering is equal parts mechanical precision and clinical empathy. I bring a student's curiosity to a professional's checklist: test it, troubleshoot it, trust it.

Outside coursework, I've spent real hours in workshops and presentation halls — sharpening how I explain complex medical-technical ideas, including mathematics-driven approaches to diagnostics, to audiences who range from engineers to clinicians.

04 / Project

Built for early detection.

IoT · Healthcare Monitoring

COPD Disease Monitoring Using IoT

An IoT-based monitoring system for Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) patients. The system tracks patient health parameters continuously to support early detection and improve ongoing disease management — turning raw sensor data into a usable clinical signal.

  • Continuous patient parameter monitoring via connected sensors
  • Early-detection logic to flag abnormal readings
  • Designed to support, not replace, clinical decision-making
